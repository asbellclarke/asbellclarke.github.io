Today’s schools and workplaces aren’t built for everyone. Some of our most brightest, most creative, and persistent problem-solvers struggle because of their executive function.

Whether because of autism, ADHD, dyslexia, stress, anxiety or depression, some of our best learners are hindered by difficulties in the ability to plan, organize, and follow through on tasks. What is Executive Function?

Executive functions are the brain processes involved with getting things done. These include…

Working Memory – keeping information we need in mind as we work on a task

Cognitive Flexibility – adjusting to new information as it presents itself

Inhibitory Control – regulating our emotions and distractions to persist on a task

Executive function controls our ability to plan, organize, collaborate, focus, and pivot as we go about our daily lives.

Executive function might be compromised by autism, ADHD, and other neurological conditions, and also by stress, anxiety, depression, or many mental health issues.

Did you ever ?

Did you ever read a paragraph and by the time you get to the last phrase, you’ve already forgotten the beginning and have to go back and read it again? And again?

Did you ever let someone down because you got so involved in a task that you forgot to show up?

Did you ever spend more time preparing for a task than actually getting it done?

These lapses in executive function can be frustrating and sometimes debilitating.
