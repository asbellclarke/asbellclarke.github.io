Neurodiversity in the Workplace

Top companies around the world increasingly look to neurodiversity for a competitive advantage. They have discovered that some of the most creative, systematic, focused, innovative, and persistent members of their workforce are autistic or have ADHD or dyslexia or more than one of these. They realize that it is not despite their differences that these employees excel, it is because of their neurodiversity.

Is it simple to change hiring and management practices to be more inclusive of neurodiversity? No. But it is doable. It's being done. And it is paying off in all kinds of ways. Let us help you and your organization open the doors to neurodivergent employees. We will work together to help you create an inclusive environment with structures that help neurodivergent thinkers thrive. Bonus: You will likely find the changes make a better workplace for everyone!

What is Neurodiversity?

Neurodiversity is the natural human variation in brain function. Brains are like fingerprints. Not two are identical. Some of our most creative, systematic, and persistent problem-solvers are autistic or have ADHD or dyslexia. Some brilliance is hampered by anxiety and depression. But when we remove the obstacles, genius can thrive.

Did you know …

Some people with autism are exceptionally talented in detailed pattern recognition, systematic thinking, and other areas critically needed for a cyberscurity or AI workforce?

Some people with ADHD are outstanding idea generators and score higher on creativity and divergent thinking tasks than non-ADHD folks.

Neurodiversity is so important to many STEM companies, they call it their competitive advantage.
